## Key Insights

### 1. Detailed Prompts Override Detail Levels
**Finding**: With a highly specific customization prompt (129 lines), the NotebookLM detail level (Concise/Standard/Detailed) has **minimal impact**.

**Evidence**:
- Timeline #2 (Concise) is more comprehensive than Timeline #7 (Detailed)
- Visual density determined more by prompt specificity than setting

**Implication**: For future infographics, **use Concise or Standard** detail level with detailed prompts for best results.

---

### 2. Less Is More (Tufte Validated)
**Finding**: The cleanest variation (#2) is the most effective.

**Evidence**:
- Timeline #2 scores highest despite being "Concise"
- Timeline #7 (most comprehensive) scores lowest
- Viewer comprehension inversely correlated with text density

**Implication**: Resist temptation to include everything. **One clear message > comprehensive data dump**.

---

### 3. Analytical Insights > Raw Data
**Finding**: Timeline #2's "An Adoption Gap Emerges" callout adds more value than listing additional milestones.

**Evidence**: This insight:
- Helps viewer interpret the diverging lines
- Adds analytical layer beyond raw percentages
- Creates memorable takeaway

**Implication**: For future infographics, **include 1-2 analytical insights rather than exhaustive data points**.

---

### 4. Festival Context Is Essential
**Finding**: Stage icons and crowd silhouettes make infographics distinctly EventAI (not generic business charts).

**Evidence**: All variations feel "festival-focused" due to these elements

**Implication**: Continue requiring festival-specific visual metaphors in prompts.

---

### 5. White Space = Strategic Emphasis
**Finding**: Generous white space (Timeline #2) draws eye to key elements (95% endpoint, phase transitions).

**Evidence**: In cluttered variations (#7, #9), no single element dominates attention

**Implication**: **Intentionally design negative space** to direct viewer focus.
