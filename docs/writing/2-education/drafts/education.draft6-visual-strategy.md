- Understand predictive analytics can forecast attendance
- Know AI chatbots can handle customer service
- Aware RFID and facial recognition technologies exist

**What You Actually Need:**

**Functional Competencies:**
- Deploy AI tools appropriately for content creation, scheduling, and analytics
- Interpret data visualizations and predictive model outputs
- Select appropriate AI applications for specific festival challenges
- Troubleshoot common AI tool failures

**Critical Competencies:**
- Evaluate vendor claims using evidence-based frameworks (baseline comparisons, independent validation, sample sizes)
- Assess regulatory compliance (GDPR, BIPA, CCPA) when AI systems process personal data
- Audit AI systems for demographic bias and fairness
- Articulate ethical trade-offs (efficiency vs. privacy, personalization vs. filter bubbles, automation vs. employment)
- Advocate for responsible AI deployment aligned with organizational values

1. **Three-column comparison:** "Current Training" (narrow, functional only) vs. "Functional Competencies" vs. "Critical Competencies" showing the gap

3. **T-shaped professional:** Vertical bar (functional depth) + horizontal bar (critical breadth) showing dual competency model

**Suggested caption:**
> *Figure 4: Current training provides surface-level tool awareness, but the industry demands dual competency: functional skills (deploy, interpret, select tools) paired with critical skills (evaluate claims, assess compliance, audit bias, advocate for values-aligned deployment).*
